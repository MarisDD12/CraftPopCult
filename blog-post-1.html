Easy Crochet Potholder Pattern - Perfect for Beginners!

Okay, can we talk about how satisfying it is to make something you'll actually use? I'm talking about potholders that don't just sit in a drawer - ones you reach for every single day because they actually work. This woven stitch potholder has become my go-to quick project. Whether I'm making a set for myself, whipping up a last-minute housewarming gift, or just need something mindless to crochet while watching TV, this pattern delivers every time. The best part? It's genuinely beginner-friendly. If you can chain and single crochet, you can make this. No complicated stitches, no confusing pattern repeats - just straightforward, satisfying crochet that works up in under an hour.

Why I Love This Project

I love this project because it teaches you fundamental crochet skills while creating something actually useful for your home. Plus, they make great last-minute gifts!

What You'll Need

  • Yarn: Cotton yarn (worsted weight) - I'm using Lion Brand 24/7 Cotton which is perfect for coasters
  • Hook: Size H/8 (5.0mm) crochet hook - this is the one I use
  • Scissors and yarn needle for finishing

üí° Why cotton? Cotton is heat-resistant and absorbent, making it perfect for coasters. Acrylic can melt with hot mugs!

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Magic Ring

Start with a magic ring. If you're not familiar with this technique, check the video at timestamp 1:30 where I show you exactly how!

Step 2: Round 1

Chain 2, then work 10 half double crochet stitches into the magic ring. Pull the tail to close the ring, then slip stitch to join. (10 hdc)

Step 3: Round 2

Chain 2, work 2 half double crochet in each stitch around. Slip stitch to join. (20 hdc)

Step 4: Round 3

Chain 2, then work: *1 hdc in next stitch, 2 hdc in next stitch* - repeat around. Slip stitch to join. (30 hdc)

Step 5: Finishing Edge

Chain 1, single crochet in each stitch around. Slip stitch to join. This creates a nice clean edge!

Step 6: Weave in Ends

Fasten off and use your yarn needle to weave in all ends. Block your coasters (just wet them and lay flat to dry) for a professional finish.

Pro Tips

  • Make sure your stitches aren't too tight - coasters should lie flat
  • Use two strands of yarn together for extra thickness
  • Make a set of 4-6 for a complete gift set
  • Try variegated yarn for easy color changes!

Common Questions

How much yarn do I need?

Each coaster uses about 20-25 yards. One skein of cotton yarn (around 200 yards) will make 6-8 coasters.

Can I use acrylic yarn?

I don't recommend it - acrylic can melt or warp with hot mugs. Stick with cotton or bamboo yarn for heat resistance.

My coaster is curling - what's wrong?

This usually means your stitches are too tight. Try loosening your tension or go up one hook size. Blocking also helps!
